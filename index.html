#1 Monaco Grand Prix - Circuit De Monaco

The Monaco Grand Prix is the most iconic Formula 1 race of the season. This tight-cornered circuit occupies most of the country, giving citizens and visitors no option but to stop and stare. This circuit is the hardest track for drivers to navigate, making it the most interesting race of the year.

#3 Belgian Grand Prix - Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps

The Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps was using public roads in the Belgian towns of Francorchamps, Malmedy, and Stavelot. This tracks ability to combine long straights, fast corners, and changes in elevation makes this beautiful track a joy to watch.

#4 British Grand Prix - Silverstone Circuit

The Silverstone Circuit is one of the most iconic tracks on the calendar. Due to the sport's popularity in Britain, and the fact that many teams are based in Britain, this track has been informally dubbes "The Home of Formula 1." This track allows drivers to push their car's to the limit with its barreling straights, and fast corners.

#5 Italian Grand Prix - Autodromo Nazionale Monza

The Italian Circuit in Monza is one of the fastest tracks of the year. This track allows drivers to be on full throttle for 80% of the drive. This track is also home to the Ferrari F1 team which attracts lots of fans to view this race. The environment of this track provides a great viewing experience for this race.

This race in 2021, was the first race I've watched start to finish because of the battle between Verstappen and Hamilton. Overall, this race is a very exciting watch.

#10 Hungarian Grand Prix - Hungaroring

The Hungarian Grand Prix is a track full of challenging racing. Due to the lack of straights, we see a lot of chasing, but drivers will get their opportunity to overtake on the main straight. Due to the weather of Hungary in the summer, drivers and teams have to micromanage their tires. This creates a difficult racing environment, providing us a very exciting race.
